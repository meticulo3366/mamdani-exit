Method, assumptions & what's not modeled
  • Modeled: 2026 federal brackets & standard deduction (OBBBA-adjusted, Rev. Proc. 2025-32) · FICA with the $184,500 SS wage base + 0.9% additional Medicare · progressive state brackets with state-specific deductions/exemptions · NY tax-benefit recapture (above $107,650 NYAGI your top rate phases in on all income — most calculators skip this) · NYC resident brackets · CA 1% mental-health surtax & 1.2% uncapped SDI payroll tax · MA 4% millionaire surtax · Philadelphia wage tax · federal SALT itemizing with the $40,400 cap and high-income phase-down, auto-compared to the standard deduction per city.
  • Bonus: annually, a bonus is ordinary income — the 40/60 split changes withholding timing, not the tax bill. The one real effect is first-year sourcing: NY taxes a bonus earned from NY workdays even if paid after you move. The toggle models this net of your new state's resident credit.
  • Not modeled: AMT · CT bracket-benefit recapture (adds up to ~$3.2k for CT high earners) · IL/CT exemption phase-outs · NJ/NY small payroll deductions (PFL/UI, <0.6% capped) · PA taxing 401(k) deferrals · local sales & property tax differences beyond your input · moving costs · capital gains and investment income (WA taxes those, not wages).
  • Regional & rural presets (1–1.5 hr out): commuter-belt and satellite towns for each metro, with commuter-rail pass defaults where relevant (Metro-North/NJ Transit monthlies run $400–530). The NYC belt matters most: Beacon keeps NY state tax but sheds the ~3.9% NYC city tax; Princeton/Asbury Park/New Haven switch you to NJ/CT rates — unless you keep the NYC job, in which case NY's convenience-of-employer rule taxes those wages anyway (that's the toggle; you still shed the city tax). Lancaster note: PA's 3.07% flat rate assumes you don't work in Philadelphia — city wage tax applies to anyone working there, resident or not.
  • Cars: each city has a car count (only NYC and a few transit-strong cities default below one per household) × your all-in per-car cost, plus a monthly transit-pass figure (NYC $132 unlimited MetroCard; editable per city in the JSON). Car insurance varies a lot by state — FL, NY, and LA metro run high, so adjust the per-car cost if insurance quotes differ materially.
  • Rents and cost indexes are placeholders — the tax math is exact to the data; the living-cost side is only as good as what you plug in.
  • Not tax advice. The NY bonus-sourcing rules and move-timing are genuinely worth a CPA — mistiming a large bonus around a move can cost more than any modeling error here.
